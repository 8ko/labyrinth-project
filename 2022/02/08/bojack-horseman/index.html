bojack horseman.

February 8, 2022February 8, 2022

bojack horseman.

major spoilers for bojack horseman. bjh is an adult cartoon available on netflix. it was recommended to me by a close friend who happens to be my good ex. adult cartoons have never been my cup of tea before bjh. i tried rick and morty and absolutely hated it. couldn’t even make it past five episodes. i initially couldn’t stomach bojack too, but he really put in a good word for this show, despite knowing how mentally draining it is to power through each episode. i think season one does a great job of introducing the characters and the anthropomorphic world, gives you a good idea of what the show’s about— bojack’s misadventures and the repercussion of his actions. shit doesn’t really hit the fan until the second season. it’s what hooked me to this series.

this series is a dive on mental illness, alcoholism, drug use, and generational trauma. and the impact it’s had on me after finishing the final season? fuck. i will take these feelings to my grave. to add, here is a spotify podcast of bojack’s most felt quotes. his words are sure to leave you in pieces because he’s a piece of shit and you naturally, you empathize with him and realize just how shitty you are and you’re no better just because he’s a fictional character and you’re not.

my favorite has got to be track three and thirteen. when i’m with you, i don’t hate myself. twenty one years of existing in this plane of existence has got me a pretty good grasp of the unfair reality and my self-identity. this quote just hit because in every relationship i’ve had, platonic or romantic, i’ve always hidden a part of myself, and hated it. this isn’t to say that it’s bad to only show specific sides of yourself to your people, but rather, i longed to find someone who made me feel myself and okay at the same time, genuinely. let me tell you, that shit is hard to find. and i did. i’m glad i did. i always wish to have loved them better when i still had them.

the view from halfway down, track thirteen, is a great fucking pre-final episode. to quote the reviewer, “The View from Halfway Down is one of the most incredible half hours of television I’ve ever seen. It’s easily the best episode of BoJack Horseman Season 6, and arguably of the entire series. In this video I breakdown all of the bits, pieces, callbacks and references that make it so incredible, while analyzing the overall message, and what it means for BoJack Horseman as a character AND as a TV series.”

alternatively, here is a… touching video of a child innocently asking her mother questions that would make more sense to the child when they’re older. the whole reason bojack is so fucked up is because his parents were fucked up and everyone else who came before his parents were fucked up. it’s generational trauma at this point. and as someone who has a rough relationship with my parents as well, not to overshare or anything, this made me feel vulnerable. you can feel the relief and tenderness and love in the mother’s eyes. if only bojack had that. if only i had that.

but then again, as you become an adult, it stops being the world’s responsibility to dance around your traumas and accommodate you. it becomes your responsibility to work through your issues and learn healthy coping mechanisms, because really, the world doesn’t owe you shit. anyone, no matter what they’ve been through, can always do better. because that what humanity means.
